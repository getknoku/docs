# Protect the widget with Turnstile

The widget endpoint accepts requests from any origin you allowlist on the **Domains** page. That gates the browser side; it doesn't gate someone scripting `curl` against your project ID. To require a verification token on every chat and feedback request, connect your own Cloudflare Turnstile site to your Knoku project.

Knoku does not host Turnstile or bill for it. You bring your own free Cloudflare account; the verification call goes from the Knoku backend to Cloudflare's `siteverify` endpoint with your secret key.

## When you should set this up

Turnstile is optional. The widget works without it. Reasons to turn it on:

- The chat endpoint is hit by traffic you can't explain in analytics.
- Your usage spikes without matching pageviews — a sign someone is scripting against your project ID.
- You're approaching your monthly message quota faster than expected.

If you launched recently and traffic is low, you can leave Turnstile off and watch for the signals above.

## Setup

1. Open **Cloudflare Turnstile** in your Cloudflare dashboard. The product is free and lives at <a href="https://dash.cloudflare.com/?to=/:account/turnstile" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">dash.cloudflare.com → Turnstile</a>. You don't need to move your DNS — Turnstile is account-scoped, not domain-scoped.
2. Click **Add site**. Pick any name. Leave **Widget mode** on the default (`Managed`).
3. Under **Hostnames**, list every domain where the Knoku widget is embedded. Match the values on your project's **Domains** page exactly — apex vs. subdomain matters.
4. Cloudflare returns a **site key** and a **secret key**. Copy both.
5. In Knoku, open your project → **Settings → Security**. Paste the keys and click **Enable Turnstile**.

The widget bundle picks up the site key on its next reload via `/api/v1/config/{projectId}` and starts attaching a verification token to every chat and feedback request. No code change on your site.

## Verifying it works

Open the widget on your live site and send a message. In your browser's DevTools Network tab, the request to `/api/v1/chat` should carry a `cf-turnstile-response` header.

To confirm the gate is enforced, try the same request without the header — for example with `curl` and your project ID. The response should be `403 {"error":"turnstile_failed"}`.

## Strict CSP sites

If your site sends a Content-Security-Policy header, allow `challenges.cloudflare.com` for the three directives Turnstile loads under:

```
script-src   https://challenges.cloudflare.com
frame-src    https://challenges.cloudflare.com
connect-src  https://challenges.cloudflare.com
```

Without these, the Turnstile script fails to load and the widget can't issue a token — every chat request will fail.

## Rotating the secret key

Paste a new secret key into **Settings → Security** and save. The previous value is overwritten in place; existing visitors don't need to reload because tokens are short-lived per request.

If you ever lose access to your Cloudflare account, click **Disable Turnstile** on the same page. The project reverts to the legacy widget behavior (no verification gate, origin allowlist only).

## What gets sent to Cloudflare

When Turnstile is enabled, every chat and feedback request from your widget triggers one call to `https://challenges.cloudflare.com/turnstile/v0/siteverify` from the Knoku backend. The request body carries your secret key, the visitor's one-time token, and the visitor's IP. Cloudflare uses this signal to score whether the request is human; the score is not persisted by Knoku.

This is a Cloudflare service governed by their own terms. Reference it in your privacy policy if you keep one — most customers list Cloudflare under "service providers" or "sub-processors".
